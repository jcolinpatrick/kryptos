## 1. SANBORN LETTER TO CIA EMPLOYEES (December 15, 1989)

**Source**: CIA declassified document C00042168, released January 1993.
**Images**: `reference/Pictures/letter1.jpg`, `reference/Pictures/letter2.jpg`
**Fax header on page 2**: "'91 01:18PM CIA/PUBLIC AFFAIRS P.6"

### Full Transcription

> December 15, 1989
>
> Dear Agency Employees:
>
> I am writing this letter to give you an idea of what I am up to at
> the Agency, and to explain those big tilted slabs of stone.
>
> The stonework in the courtyard and at the entrance to the new
> building serves two functions.
>
> First, it creates a natural framework for the project as a
> whole and is part of a landscaping scheme designed to
> recall the natural stone outcroppings that existed on this
> site before the Agency, and that will endure as do
> mountains.
>
> Second, the tilted strata tell a story like pages of a
> document. Over the next several months, a flat copper
> sheet through which letters and symbols are cut will be
> inserted between these stone "pages." This code, which
> includes certain ancient ciphers, begins as International
> Morse and increases in complexity as you move through
> the piece at the entrance and into the courtyard. Its
> placement in a geologic context reinforces the text's
> "hiddenness" as if it were a fossil or an image frozen in time.
>
> An installation in the courtyard further explores this theme. On
> the paved surface, supported by a petrified tree, will stand a
> curved, vertical copper plate. Approximately 2000 letters of the
> alphabet are cut through this plate (a process which requires four
> months of work). The left side of the plate is a table for deciphering
> and enciphering code, developed by Blaise de Vigenere in 1570.
>
> [PAGE 2]
>
> The right side is a text that can be partly deciphered by using the
> table and partly by using a potentially challenging encoding
> system. The text, written in collaboration with a prominent fiction
> writer, is revealed only after the code is deciphered.
>
> My choice of materials, like code, conveys meaning. At the
> entrance, a lodestone (a rock naturally magnetized by lighting)
> refers to ancient navigational compasses. The petrified tree recalls
> the trees that once stood on this site and that were the source of
> materials on which written language has been recorded. The
> copper, perforated by text, represents this "paper." I also use
> another symbol; water. In a small pool on the plaza, partly
> surrounded by the copper plate, water will be turbulent and
> provocative, constantly agitated into standing waves. In the
> other pool, located among trees in the courtyard and between two
> massive outcroppings, water will be calm, reflective,
> contemplative. Other materials around the site - large stones,
> ornamental grasses, and small trees - are designed to make the
> natural features surrounding the Agency more visually
> interesting and thought provoking.
>
> My work at Langley is approximately two thirds complete. If you
> see me or my apprentices working, please don't hesitate to ask
> questions about the work.
>
> Sincerely,
> Jim Sanborn

### KEY CLUES FROM THE 1989 LETTER

1. **"partly deciphered by using the table and partly by using a potentially challenging encoding system"** — Confirms two-system design from 1989, BEFORE installation. "The table" = Vigenere tableau. "Potentially challenging encoding system" = the K4 method.

2. **"written in collaboration with a prominent fiction writer"** — **[RESOLVED]** This was **John le Carré** (David Cornwell). Sanborn confirms in Smithsonian oral history: "I had originally thought to have a prominent fiction writer like John Le Carré AKA David Cornwell contribute to the text and even sent a letter to his publisher but I never got a reply." The collaboration NEVER HAPPENED. Sanborn wrote the PT himself. The 1989 letter was either aspirational or describing the original intent. See `reference/john_le_carre_dossier.md`.

3. **"the text... is revealed only after the code is deciphered"** — The plaintext has literary/narrative significance beyond just being a message.

4. **"increases in complexity as you move through the piece"** — Confirms progressive difficulty: Morse → K1/K2 (Vigenere) → K3 (transposition) → K4 (hardest).

5. **"Approximately 2000 letters"** — The total letter count on the cipher side.

6. **Date: December 15, 1989** — Written 11 months BEFORE the November 3, 1990 dedication. The sculpture was ~2/3 complete. This means the cipher design was finalized by late 1989.

### KEY CLUES FROM THE ORAL HISTORY

1. **"These sheets are really composed of four separate copper sheets"** — The cipher text side is FOUR physical copper sheets. This could correspond to K1, K2, K3, K4 being on separate physical sheets.

2. **"I used that table to encipher the top plate"** — The Vigenere tableau was used for the "top plate." This likely means K1+K2 (the upper portion of the cipher side).

3. **"The bottom plate is enciphered in a much more difficult fashion"** — K3+K4 (the bottom) uses a DIFFERENT, harder method.

4. **"There are two systems of enciphering the bottom text"** — THE MOST CRITICAL QUOTE. Two systems for the bottom text. This could mean: (a) K3 uses one system (transposition) and K4 uses another, OR (b) K4 itself uses two systems layered together.

5. **"two separate systems and that is a major clue in itself"** — Sanborn says the EXISTENCE of two systems is itself a clue.

6. **"the whole piece is designed to unveil itself, as if you were to pull up one layer, then you can come to the next"** — Layer-by-layer unveiling. Consistent with multi-layer encryption.

7. **"I haven't given anybody the key words"** — Keywords plural. The Vigenere section uses multiple keywords (we know: KRYPTOS and PALIMPSEST for K1, KRYPTOS and ABSCISSA for K2).

8. **"at sort of the eleventh hour"** — This interview/talk happened near the dedication. Someone asked about two systems only at the last moment.

9. **"the two and a half years of working on the piece"** — Confirms ~2.5 year creation timeline (roughly 1988-1990).

10. **"changed over the two and a half years"** — The concept evolved during creation. The original plan changed.

## 8. ANALYSIS AND IMPLICATIONS

### What the 1989 letter tells us NEW:

1. **"Prominent fiction writer"** — Who co-wrote the plaintext? Not Ed Scheidt (he's a cryptographer). Could be a novelist who helped adapt the Carter tomb narrative. This is a NAMED but UNIDENTIFIED collaborator. Research needed.

2. **Timing**: Letter dated Dec 1989, sculpture ~2/3 done. The cipher design was SET by this point. The "potentially challenging encoding system" language suggests K4's method was already decided.

3. **"Approximately 2000 letters"** — Total count. K1(63)+K2(369)+K3(336)+K4(97)+tableau(~900) ≈ ~1765 cipher+tableau letters. With Morse code section letters, reaches ~2000.

### What the oral history tells us NEW:

1. **"Four separate copper sheets"** — Physical structure matters. If K4 is on its own sheet, the boundaries are physical, not just logical.

2. **"The bottom plate... much more difficult"** — Confirms K3+K4 use harder methods than K1+K2.

3. **"Two systems"** — This is the canonical "two separate systems" quote. Context: he's talking about "the bottom text" which is the lower half of the cipher side. This could mean K3 (transposition) + K4 (something else), OR K4 alone uses two layered systems.

### What the yellow pad tells us NEW:

1. **K4 plaintext is the Carter/Tutankhamun narrative** — "Slowly desperately slowly the remains of passage debris..." through "Can you see anything? Yes wonderful things." This has been suspected but the pad CONFIRMS it.

2. **The editing process is visible** — Sanborn was drafting and revising. Words were changed, spellings corrected. The "desparetly" and "debbris" misspellings appear to be deliberate or at least pre-existing in his working draft.

3. **"8 Lines 73"** — If this refers to K4, the number 73 is extremely interesting. 97 - 24 (known crib chars) = 73. OR K4 was originally 73 chars. OR the plaintext before padding/delimiters is 73 chars.

4. **The plaintext draft does NOT contain "EASTNORTHEAST" or "BERLINCLOCK"** — These cribs must come from the ENCRYPTED form or from a different layer/section that connects K4 to coordinates and Berlin.

### Open questions for further research:
