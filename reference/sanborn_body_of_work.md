# Jim Sanborn — Body of Work Analysis (2026-03-07)

## Source: jimsanborn.net + Elonka archives + news sources

## The "Kryptos Decoding Filter" (1994)
- Part of "Filter Media" exhibition at **Nancy Drysdale Gallery**, Washington, D.C., 1994
- **Aluminum, brass, copper, shadow, 51"h x 17"di** — likely cylindrical (17"di = diameter)
- Explicitly named "Kryptos Decoding Filter" — Greek for "hidden"
- Paired with "CBET - Decoding Filter" (same dimensions, Russian for "light")
- **NOT a literal decoder** — an optical/conceptual artwork related to decoding
- Source: Elonka's list (from Nancy Drysdale files via Nadine Granoff). NOT on current official site.

### 1994 Washington Post Review (Lee Fleming, "The Shadow Shows", Sept 30 1994)
PRIMARY SOURCE description of the Filter Media exhibition:
- **"metal sheets that have been pierced repeatedly by the letters of a particular word"**
- **"punch-outs reduce the metal to a kind of dense mesh, so that the words are virtually unreadable"**
- **"when a light shines through the mesh, legible shadows of the letters are cast onto the floor or wall"**
- Pieces "look simple, resembling **skinny tables or small folded screens**" — NOT described as cylinders
- Made of "rods, lenses, pierced copper sheets and, in one case, a telescope"
- **"light projections add layers of shadows"** and "compel viewers to notice small details"
- "Deceit Filters" = "imaginary way to filter out duplicity" — conceptual, not functional
- **The "Decoding" and "Minutia" filters are described as "larger"** but grouped with Deceit Filters
- Inclinare/Aequus: THREE metal sheets stacked vertically on rods. Top sheet has cut words.
  Light projects through top sheet onto tilted middle sheet, then through mesh square onto level bottom sheet.
  **Multiple layers of projection through stacked perforated sheets.**

**Key insight**: Sanborn's concept of "decoding" is PHYSICAL FILTERING — light passes through
perforated/cut metal sheets, projecting text as shadow. Conceptually related to a Cardan grille,
but the WaPo review makes clear these are **artistic metaphors**, not functional decoders.
The Inclinare piece demonstrates that MULTIPLE STACKED layers of filtering can be involved.
**CAUTION**: "cylinder" inference for 51"h×17"di is reasonable but unconfirmed by the review,
which describes pieces as "skinny tables or small folded screens."

## Cyrillic Projector (1997, UNC Charlotte)
- Bronze cylinder with Cyrillic text cut through walls
- **Keyword: ТЕНЬ (TEHb) = "SHADOW"** — the shadow theme IS the key
- Uses Vigenere cipher with Cyrillic alphabet
- Two "pages": cipher side + Vigenere tableau side
- **MEDUSA** embedded in Latin characters (anomaly)
- Solved 2003 by Frank Corr and Mike Bales
- Content: KGB agent psychological control instructions + Sakharov correspondence

## Projection Cylinder Works (post-Kryptos)
All use: bronze/copper cylinder, waterjet-cut text, pinpoint light source inside.
Light projects through cut letters onto surrounding surfaces.

- **Code Room (1990/1992)**: "Bronze cylinder with Kryptos Vigenere cipher" — actual K4 text!
- **Lux (2001)**: "Letters incised with water jet cutter, bathe sidewalk in lighted letters at night"
- **Radiance (2008)**: "Water-jet cut passages of text in many languages"
- **Lingua (2002)**: Two 16'×12' cylinders, 8 languages
- **Meridian**: Denver Union Station, 12'×5' cylinder
- **Bright Stars Memorial (2023)**: Granite amphitheater + 12' projection cylinder

**Concept**: Text on curved surface, non-linear reading order, message revealed through projection.

- Granite, quartz, lodestone, copper, Chinese text, water channel — **IDENTICAL materials to Kryptos**

## Archeo-Geometry Series (2021-2025) — Current Work
- 3D bronze pieces based on Euclid's "Elements" and Descartes' Cartesian coordinates
- **"Shadows complete form and intent"** — the shadow IS the artwork
- Series of twelve pieces: Euclidean and Cartesian forms
- Geometry + shadow = spatial transformation visible only through projection

## Thematic Synthesis for K4
1. **FILTER = GRILLE**: Sanborn's dominant metaphor for decoding is physical filtering
2. **SHADOW = REVELATION**: The message is in the shadow/projection, not the surface
3. **CYLINDER = NON-LINEAR READING**: Text on curved surfaces has non-obvious reading order
4. **LODESTONE = MISDIRECTION**: Overrides normal navigation; points you away from true north
5. **PHYSICAL > MATHEMATICAL**: All encoding/decoding is spatial/geometric, not algorithmic
