# William H. Webster — Kryptos Reference

## Relevance to Kryptos

William Hedgcock Webster (March 6, 1924 – August 8, 2025) was the Director of Central Intelligence from May 26, 1987 to August 31, 1991. **Kryptos was commissioned, designed, and installed during his tenure as CIA Director.** The sculpture was dedicated on November 3, 1990.

Webster's initials "WW" appear in the K2 plaintext:
> "WHO KNOWS THE EXACT LOCATION ONLY **WW** THIS IS HIS LAST MESSAGE"

This makes Webster the only living person directly referenced in the Kryptos plaintext (until his death in 2025). The phrase "THIS IS HIS LAST MESSAGE" takes on additional resonance after his passing.

### Timeline intersection with 2025 Kryptos events
- **August 8, 2025**: Webster dies at 101 in Warrenton, Virginia
- **August 2025**: Sanborn releases open letter with 4 new clues (same month)
- **September 2025**: Kobek/Byrne discover K4 plaintext strips at Smithsonian
- **October 16, 2025**: NYT publishes Kobek/Byrne story
- **November 20, 2025**: $962,500 auction of complete K4 archive

Whether Webster's death prompted Sanborn's unprecedented disclosure is unconfirmed but the timing is notable.

### Antipodes W.W. anomaly
On the Antipodes sculpture (Hirshhorn Museum), the K2 position where Kryptos has "WW" shows "S.F." with two dots — the ONLY periods on either sculpture. On Row 22 of Antipodes, the question mark before "ONLY" is made unusually skinny to accommodate the dots. Sanborn considered the dots more important than consistent punctuation spacing.

## Biography

### Early life and education
- Born March 6, 1924, St. Louis, Missouri
- Son of Thomas Milliken Webster and Katherine Hedgcock
- Raised in Webster Groves, Missouri
- U.S. Navy lieutenant, World War II
- B.A., Amherst College, 1947 (member of Psi Upsilon fraternity)
- J.D., Washington University School of Law, 1949
- Served in Navy again during Korean War
- Joined Armstrong Teasdale law firm, St. Louis
- U.S. Attorney, Eastern District of Missouri, 1960–1961
- Missouri Board of Law Examiners, 1964–1969

### Federal judicial career
- **U.S. District Judge**, Eastern District of Missouri (1970–1973) — nominated by Nixon
- **U.S. Circuit Judge**, Eighth Circuit Court of Appeals (1973–1978) — nominated by Nixon
- Was on Gerald Ford's shortlist for Supreme Court in 1975 (seat went to John Paul Stevens)
- Attorney General Edward Levi called him "a very competent judge — energetic, careful, and intelligent"

### Director of the FBI (1978–1987)
- Appointed by President Jimmy Carter (despite Webster being a registered Republican)
- Assumed office February 23, 1978
- Served 9 years, rebuilding morale and restoring institutional relationships
- Portrayed by actor Sean Cullen in Netflix's *Mindhunter* (Season 2)

### Director of Central Intelligence (1987–1991)
- Appointed by President Ronald Reagan
- **Served May 26, 1987 – August 31, 1991** (4 years, 97 days — 5th-longest of 19 directors)
- Tenure: 97 days beyond 4 years. K4 is 97 characters. [COINCIDENCE — noted but unverified]
- Took over CIA during Iran-Contra fallout; worked to restore agency credibility
- **Kryptos sculpture commissioned, designed, and dedicated (Nov 3, 1990) during his directorship**
- Only person to have led both the FBI and CIA
- After CIA, practiced law at Milbank (arbitration, mediation, internal investigation)

### Later career
- First chairman, Public Company Accounting Oversight Board (2002) — resigned after controversy
- Chairman, Homeland Security Advisory Council (2005–2020)
- Endorsed Biden (2020) and Harris (2024) over Trump, signing letters with 100+ Republican national security officials

### Personal life
- Married Drusilla Lane (34 years, until her death in 1984); three children
- Married Lynda Clugston in 1990
- In 2015, he and Clugston were targeted by a Jamaican lottery scammer who threatened arson and sniper attacks; perpetrator sentenced to nearly 6 years (2019)
- Turned 100 on March 6, 2024
- Died August 8, 2025, at a care facility in Warrenton, Virginia, age 101

### Honors (selected)
- Distinguished Intelligence Medal (1991)
- Presidential Medal of Freedom (1991)
- National Security Medal (1991)
- U.S. Senator John Heinz Award for Greatest Public Service (1984)
- William J. Donovan Award, OSS Society (2005)
- Golden Plate Award, American Academy of Achievement (1978)
- Honorary doctorates from Institute of World Politics, National Intelligence University, and others
- Washington University School of Law created the "Webster Society" outstanding scholars program (1999)
