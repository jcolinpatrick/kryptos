K4 KEYED COLUMNAR TRANSPOSITION TEST RESULTS
Date: 2026-03-03
================================================================================

OBJECTIVE:
Test whether K4 uses keyed columnar transposition (like K3) with different
widths and keywords than K3's confirmed (21, 28) RTL method.

TEST COVERAGE:
1. Keyed columnar with padding at widths 2-97
2. Keywords tested: KRYPTOS, PALIMPSEST, ABSCISSA, BERLINCLOCK
3. Substitution layers: Vigenère, Beaufort (pre and post transposition)
4. Alphabets: Standard AZ and Kryptos-keyed KA
5. Simple columnar transposition (LTR, RTL, spiral) as fallback

SCRIPTS CREATED:
- e_k4_keyed_columnar_01.py
- e_k4_keyed_columnar_02.py
- e_k4_keyed_columnar_03.py (with padding)
- e_k4_simple_columnar_04.py (simple transposition)

RESULTS:
========

KEYED COLUMNAR TESTS: 0 crib matches
- Tested all widths 2-97 with padding to make divisible
- 4 keywords × 4 padding chars × 3 substitution keywords × 2 alphabets
- Transposition → Substitution AND Substitution → Transposition
- Result: NO CRIBS FOUND (EASTNORTHEAST, BERLINCLOCK, SLOWLY, etc.)

SIMPLE COLUMNAR TESTS: 0 crib matches
- Tested all divisible widths and ALL widths with padding
- LTR, RTL, and spiral column reading orders
- Combined with Vig/Beaufort
- Result: NO CRIBS FOUND

CONCLUSION:
===========

K4 is NOT encrypted using:
✗ Keyed columnar transposition with keywords KRYPTOS/PALIMPSEST/ABSCISSA/BERLINCLOCK
✗ Simple columnar transposition with standard LTR/RTL reading
✗ Standard Vigenère or Beaufort substitution alone or combined with columnar

K4 MUST use:
1. A DIFFERENT type of transposition (rail fence, route, boustrophedon, etc.)
2. A DIFFERENT keyword (not tested)
3. A DIFFERENT substitution system (not Vigenère/Beaufort)
4. A completely novel/bespoke encryption method
5. Multiple layers in a different configuration

NEXT INVESTIGATION AREAS:
=========================
- Route transposition (various routes)
- Rail fence cipher at multiple depths
- Playfair or other polyalphabetic systems
- Plaintext-dependent substitution
- Physical/procedural ciphers (Gillogly: "never in crypto literature")
- Alternative positions for cribs (not at indices 21-33, 63-73)

CONSTRAINT NOTES:
=================
- 97 is prime (only divides by 1 and 97)
- 434 = 2×7×31 (K3+?+K4 total length)
- GCD(21,28)=7 was key to K3 method, but K4 may not follow same pattern
- Cribs may appear at different positions if K4 plaintext structure differs from assumed
