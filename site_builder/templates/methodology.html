Methodology

This page explains how we test theories, score results, and determine whether a cipher approach has been eliminated.

The K4 Ciphertext

K4 consists of 97 characters. All 26 letters of the English alphabet appear at least once. The ciphertext, with known plaintext positions highlighted:

{{ ct[:21] }}{{ ct[21:34] }}{{ ct[34:63] }}{{ ct[63:74] }}{{ ct[74:] }}

Known plaintext (0-indexed positions, confirmed by Jim Sanborn):

  • Positions 21–33: EASTNORTHEAST (13 characters)
  • Positions 63–73: BERLINCLOCK (11 characters)

These 24 known characters are the foundation of our scoring system. They also produce known keystream values at those positions, which constrain what cipher methods and keys are possible.

Scoring: Crib Matching (0–24)

For any proposed decryption method and key, we check how many of the 24 known plaintext positions produce the correct letter. This gives a score from 0 to 24.

ScoreClassificationMeaning
0–9NOISEExpected performance from a random key/method. Not recorded.
10–17INTERESTINGAbove noise floor. Recorded for completeness, but almost certainly coincidental.
18–23SIGNALStatistically unusual. Warrants investigation, but may be a false positive at high periods.
24FULL MATCHAll 24 cribs match. Requires Bean constraint validation, quadgram analysis, and human review before any claim is made.

Expected Random Scores by Period

A critical subtlety: the expected random score depends on the cipher's period (key length). At higher periods, random keys match more cribs simply due to underdetermination — there are fewer constraints per key position.

PeriodExpected Random ScoreDiscriminative?
2–7~8.2 / 24Yes — meaningful range
8~9.6 / 24Marginally
13~13.5 / 24Weak
17~17.3 / 24No — random matches 17+
19~18.1 / 24No
24~19.2 / 24No — random matches 19+
26~20.0 / 24No

Key insight: Only scores at period ≤ 7 are meaningful discriminators. All high scores at period ≥ 17 are false positives. This is a mathematical property of underdetermination, not a limitation of our testing.

Bean Constraints

In 2021, Richard Bean published additional constraints on the K4 keystream derived from the repeated letter P at positions 27 and 65 of the ciphertext (which decrypt to R at both positions). This gives:

  • Equality: k[27] = k[65] (the key values at these positions must be equal)
  • 242 Variant-Independent Inequalities: pairs of key positions that must differ under all three cipher variants (Vigenère, Beaufort, Variant Beaufort). Derived from all C(24,2) = 276 crib pairs, minus 13 equalities and 21 mixed pairs.

These constraints are variant-independent — they hold whether the cipher is Vigenère, Beaufort, or Variant Beaufort. Any valid solution must satisfy all Bean constraints. The full 242-pair inequality set eliminates all periods 1–26 for periodic substitution on the raw 97-character carved text.

Two-System Model

Jim Sanborn confirmed at the Kryptos dedication that “there are two systems of enciphering the bottom text”. Combined with the mathematical impossibility of single-layer periodic substitution, this means K4 uses a product cipher — two layers of encryption applied in sequence.

Statistical analysis (digram frequency test) confirms the outermost layer is substitution, not transposition. This gives the encryption order: 

  PT → transposition → substitution → carved text

Our current attack strategy tests this model: undo columnar transposition, then check crib consistency for the substitution layer. We have exhaustively tested:

  • All 362,880 width-9 column permutations × Vig/Beau/VBeau × AZ/KA × periods 1–13 + autokey
  • Keyword-derived column orders for widths 4–14 × all substitution types
  • Running-key decryption from Howard Carter’s book and K1–K3 plaintext (crib-dragged)

All tests produced zero signal, eliminating standard columnar transposition × periodic substitution as the K4 method.

Validation Criteria

A candidate solution is not accepted unless it passes all of the following simultaneously:

  • Crib score: 24/24
  • Bean constraints: PASS
  • Quadgram log-probability: > −4.8 per character (English-like text quality)
  • Index of coincidence consistent with English (IC ≈ 0.067 expected)
  • Semantic coherence: reads as meaningful English with recognizable words (human review required)

Truth Taxonomy

Every claim in our database is classified by its evidence level:

[PUBLIC FACT]
Verified by reputable public reporting or primary-source statements.
[DERIVED FACT]
Deterministic consequence of public facts, reproducible by a provided command.
[INTERNAL RESULT]
Empirical result from this project. Includes artifact pointers and a reproduction command.
[HYPOTHESIS]
Plausible claim not yet proven. Includes a test plan.

Confidence Tiers

Tier 1 — Mathematical Proof (~99.9%)
Algebraic proof that the method cannot produce the known plaintext. Permanently valid unless crib positions or ciphertext transcription are wrong.
Tier 2 — Exhaustive Search (~95%)
Every possible configuration was tested under stated assumptions. Solid for the specific model tested, but does not eliminate multi-layer variants.
Tier 3 — Partial/Statistical (~40–70%)
Sampling-based or incomplete coverage. May warrant re-testing under different assumptions.
Tier 4 — Untested (~0%)
Never properly tested. Fully open.

Reproducibility

Every elimination in our database includes a reproduction command that you can run yourself. The entire codebase is open source. Clone the repo, install Python 3.11+, and run any experiment with PYTHONPATH=src.
